NOTICE OF DECISION ISSUED UNDER PROTECTIVE ORDER

 

Attached is our decision resolving the above-referenced protest(s).  The decision is being issued as a protected document and may be released only as permitted by the terms of the protective order.  We will subsequently issue a public version, which will carry the legend:  'Document for Public Release'.  ONLY that document may be released publicly.  Be advised that the public version may also contain editorial changes.  Until this version has been released, counsel may advise their clients of the outcome of the protest(s) consistent with their obligations under the protective order.

 

To facilitate our preparation of the public version of the decision, the parties should attempt to agree upon a mutually acceptable, proposed redacted version and submit that version to our Office within three business days of the date of the decision.  Text proposed for redaction in the version furnished to GAO should not be completely obscured; rather, such text should be identified using grey scale highlighting, brackets, or other means.  The parties should separately identify any portion of information upon which they cannot agree.  The parties proposed redactions themselves are protected and may not be released to individuals not admitted under the protective order.  Please affirmatively indicate if the parties are proposing that no redactions be made to the decision.

 

In proposing redactions, please be aware that GAO balances the propriety of any proposed redaction with our practice of producing publicly available decisions.  Accordingly, when considering requests for redaction, our Office begins with a presumption of public access to our decisions.  To overcome the presumption of public release and transparency, a party seeking redaction of information must provide compelling reasons supported by factual and legal justification.  Conclusory explanations and claims are unacceptable to overcome this presumption.  Even if covered by a protective order during the protest process, information will be redacted only where it is determined to be proprietary or source-selection-sensitive and where continuing to protect the information is necessary to safeguard the competitive process.  For example, evaluation point scores, adjectival ratings, unfavorable or adverse past performance information, and total pricing generally will not be redacted from the decision.  GAO's goal is to make public a meaningful and transparent decision.
